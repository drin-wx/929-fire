COOKIE POLICY

Data Storage & Tracking Protocols

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small snippets of encrypted data stored directly on your computer or mobile hardware configuration. They allow our server layers to identify session states and adapt page layouts efficiently upon return visits.

2. How We Apply Cookies

We use functional and analytical tracking technologies to enhance user experiences. These include:


• Authentication Cookies: Retaining security tokens to keep authorized incident handlers logged into the emergency portal.

• Preference Storage: Saving customized interface selections, such as regional display scales on active emergency maps.

• Performance Auditing: Reviewing general connection behaviors to address network lag or layout failures during high-traffic typhoon alerts.

3. Managing Cookie Settings

Most desktop and mobile browsers accept tracking tokens natively. If you prefer to disable cookie tracking, you can adjust these preferences inside your personal browser settings panel. Please be informed that disabling strict operational cookies may break specific login frameworks or interactive mapping scripts on our dispatcher dashboard.
